# Attribution, provenance, and non-affiliation

## Independent status

This repository is maintained as an independent community guide. Its
maintainer is **not the creator or trainer** of the Qoruyo or Sophro Mhiro
models. The repository is not an official publication of Beth Mardutho,
Princeton University, Universite Paris Sciences & Lettres, Kraken, or Zenodo,
and no endorsement by those organizations is implied.

## Original model creators

The four Qoruyo records identify **Beth Mardutho** as creator.

The four Sophro Mhiro records identify the following creators:

- Beth Mardutho
- George Kiraz
- Christine Roughan
- Daniel Stokl Ben Ezra

The Sophro Mhiro record descriptions also acknowledge the Syriac
Transcribathon data collectors, participating institutions, manuscript
repositories, and funding sources. Consult and cite the original records for
the complete authoritative credits.

## Model licensing

At the time this guide was assembled, all eight Zenodo records declared
`CC-BY-4.0`. Under CC BY 4.0, redistribution and adaptation are permitted with
appropriate attribution, a license link, and an indication of modifications.

This repository deliberately does not redistribute the `.mlmodel` binaries.
Users retrieve them from their authoritative Zenodo records through Kraken.

## Documentation authorship

James Issa assembled and independently tested the practical documentation in
this repository. Documentation authorship should not be confused with model
authorship.

## Suggested attribution

When using a model, cite its exact Zenodo DOI and name its listed creators. For
example:

> Beth Mardutho. "Qoruyo: Syriac model for the recognition of printed text in
> Eastern Syriac script." Zenodo, 2025.
> https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17406690.

If this guide materially supported a workflow, it may be acknowledged or cited
separately using `CITATION.cff`.
