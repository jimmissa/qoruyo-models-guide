# Future Syriac OCR targets

This file records public-domain Syriac text editions that are good candidates
for future OCR work. It is intended as an acquisition and infrastructure
planning list for expanding the author-level corpora in this repository.

The working cutoff here is publication before January 1, 1931, which places
these historical editions in the public domain under current United States
rules. Copyright and access conditions can differ internationally, and modern
reprints, scans, corrected transcriptions, OCR files, or digital editions may
carry separate rights or contractual terms. Verify source permissions before
redistribution.

The term "critical edition" is used broadly: several nineteenth- and early
twentieth-century publications are scholarly Syriac text editions without
meeting modern critical-edition standards.

## Highest-value next acquisitions

1. **Aphrahat** - Jean Parisot, ed., *Aphraatis Sapientis Persae Demonstrationes*, 2 vols., Patrologia Syriaca 1-2. Paris, 1894 and 1907.
2. **Narsai** - Alphonse Mingana, ed., *Narsai Doctoris Syri Homiliae et Carmina*, 2 vols. Mosul, 1905.
3. **Philoxenus of Mabbug** - E. A. Wallis Budge, ed. and trans., *The Discourses of Philoxenus, Bishop of Mabbogh, A.D. 485-519*, 2 vols. London, 1894.
4. **Sahdona / Martyrius** - Paul Bedjan, ed., *S. Martyrii qui et Sahdona quae supersunt omnia*. Paris/Leipzig, 1902.
5. **Isho'yahb III of Adiabene** - Rubens Duval, ed. and trans., *Isho'yahb Patriarchae III Liber Epistularum*, CSCO 11-12. Paris/Leipzig, 1904-1905.
6. **Isaac of Nineveh** - Paul Bedjan, ed., *Mar Isaacus Ninivita de Perfectione Religiosa*. Leipzig, 1909.
7. **John of Ephesus** - E. W. Brooks, ed. and trans., *John of Ephesus: Lives of the Eastern Saints*, Patrologia Orientalis 17.1, 18.4, and 19.2. 1923-1926.
8. **Jacob of Edessa** - J.-B. Chabot, ed., *Iacobi Edesseni Hexaemeron, seu in Opus Creationis Libri Septem*, CSCO 92, Scriptores Syri 44. Paris, 1928.

Jacob of Serugh and the Isaac of Antioch corpus are already represented in this
repository and therefore are not listed here as next targets, though additional
Jacob material outside Bedjan's main homily volumes remains valuable.

## Author-by-author planning list

### Aphrahat

- William Wright, ed., *The Homilies of Aphraates, the Persian Sage*. London, 1869.
- Jean Parisot, ed., *Aphraatis Sapientis Persae Demonstrationes*, vol. 1, Patrologia Syriaca 1. Paris, 1894.
- Jean Parisot, ed., *Aphraatis Sapientis Persae Demonstrationes*, vol. 2, Patrologia Syriaca 2. Paris, 1907.

Best corpus source: Parisot's two-volume edition of the complete collection of
twenty-three Demonstrations. Wright remains useful as an independent earlier
edition.

### Ephrem the Syrian

- Joseph Simon Assemani, Peter Mubarak, and collaborators, eds., *Sancti Patris Nostri Ephraem Syri Opera Omnia quae exstant Graece, Syriace, Latine*, 6 vols. Rome, 1732-1746. The principal Syriac material is in vols. 4-6.
- J. J. Overbeck, ed., *S. Ephraemi Syri, Rabulae Episcopi Edesseni, Balaei aliorumque Opera Selecta*. Oxford, 1865.
- Gustav Bickell, ed., *S. Ephraemi Syri Carmina Nisibena*. Leipzig, 1866.
- T. J. Lamy, ed., *Sancti Ephraem Syri Hymni et Sermones*, 4 vols. Mechelen, 1882-1902.
- Rubens Duval, ed. and trans., "Le Testament de saint Ephrem." *Journal asiatique* 18 (1901): 234-319.
- C. W. Mitchell, ed. and trans., *S. Ephraim's Prose Refutations of Mani, Marcion, and Bardaisan*, 2 vols. London, 1912 and 1921.

Caution: these old editions contain substantial material now regarded as
doubtful, pseudonymous, misattributed, or textually superseded by later CSCO
editions. Ephrem should be encoded work by work, with modern authenticity
judgments recorded as metadata.

### Cyrillona / Qurillona

- Gustav Bickell, ed., "Die Gedichte des Cyrillonas nebst einigen anderen syrischen Inedits." *Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morgenlaendischen Gesellschaft* 27 (1873): 566-625.
- Gustav Bickell, "Berichtigungen zu Cyrillonas." *Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morgenlaendischen Gesellschaft* 35 (1881): 531-532.

This remains the principal old Syriac edition of Cyrillona's small surviving
poetic corpus.

### Balai of Qenneshrin

- J. J. Overbeck, ed., *S. Ephraemi Syri, Rabulae Episcopi Edesseni, Balaei aliorumque Opera Selecta*. Oxford, 1865.
- K. V. Zettersteen, ed. and trans., *Beitraege zur Kenntnis der religioesen Dichtung Balai's*. Leipzig, 1902.

Zettersteen is the largest old collection, but much of the material is only
traditionally attributed to Balai.

### Rabbula of Edessa

- J. J. Overbeck, ed., *S. Ephraemi Syri, Rabulae Episcopi Edesseni, Balaei aliorumque Opera Selecta*. Oxford, 1865.

Overbeck contains Rabbula's correspondence, homily, ecclesiastical material,
and related texts. The Life of Rabbula included there is about Rabbula, not by
him.

### Narsai

- Alphonse Mingana, ed., *Narsai Doctoris Syri Homiliae et Carmina*, 2 vols. Mosul, 1905.

This is the principal large public-domain edition of Narsai's metrical
homilies.

### Philoxenus of Mabbug

- Ignazio Guidi, ed., *La lettera di Filosseno ai monaci di Tell 'Adda*. Rome, 1886.
- E. A. Wallis Budge, ed. and trans., *The Discourses of Philoxenus, Bishop of Mabbogh, A.D. 485-519*, 2 vols. London, 1894.
- A. A. Vaschalde, ed. and trans., *Three Letters of Philoxenus, Bishop of Mabbogh (485-519)*. Rome, 1902.
- A. A. Vaschalde, ed. and trans., *Philoxeni Mabbugensis Tractatus Tres de Trinitate et Incarnatione*, CSCO 9-10. Paris/Leipzig, 1907.
- Francois Nau, ed., "Litterature canonique syriaque inedite ... canons ... de Philoxene..." *Revue de l'Orient chretien* 14 (1909): 1-49, 113-130.
- Joseph Lebon, ed. and trans., "Textes inedits de Philoxene de Mabboug." *Le Museon* 43 (1930): 17-84, 149-220.

### John of Tella

- T. J. Lamy, ed., *Dissertatio de Syrorum Fide et Disciplina in Re Eucharistica; Accedunt Veteris Ecclesiae Syriacae Monumenta Duo*. Louvain, 1859.
- Carol Kuberczyk, ed., *Canones Iohannis bar Cursus, Tellae Mauzlatae Episcopi*. Leipzig, 1901.

Kuberczyk is the principal dedicated edition.

### John of Ephesus

- William Cureton, ed. and trans., *The Third Part of the Ecclesiastical History of John, Bishop of Ephesus*. Oxford, 1853.
- J. P. N. Land, ed., *Joannis Episcopi Ephesi Monophysitae Scripta Historica Quotquot Adhuc Inedita Supererant*, in *Anecdota Syriaca*, vol. 2. Leiden, 1868.
- E. W. Brooks, ed. and trans., *John of Ephesus: Lives of the Eastern Saints*, Patrologia Orientalis 17.1, 18.4, and 19.2. 1923-1926.

Brooks's *Lives* prints the Syriac with English translation. Brooks's later
1935 critical edition of the Ecclesiastical History is outside the present
cutoff.

### Joshua the Stylite / Pseudo-Joshua

- J.-P.-P. Martin, ed. and trans., *Chronique de Josue le Stylite, ecrite vers l'an 515*. Leipzig, 1876.
- William Wright, ed. and trans., *The Chronicle of Joshua the Stylite, Composed in Syriac A.D. 507*. Cambridge, 1882.

The authorship is commonly treated as uncertain, so Pseudo-Joshua the Stylite
is the safer corpus label.

### Ahudemmeh

- Francois Nau, ed. and trans., *Histoires d'Ahoudemmeh et de Marouta, metropolitains jacobites de Tagrit et de l'Orient ... suivies du traite d'Ahoudemmeh sur l'homme*, Patrologia Orientalis 3.1. Issued in PO vol. 3 in 1909.

This combines hagiographic material about Ahudemmeh and Marutha with the
treatise *On Man* attributed to Ahudemmeh; it is not a homogeneous authorial
corpus.

### Stephen bar Sudayli / Pseudo-Hierotheos

- F. S. Marsh, ed. and trans., *The Book Which Is Called the Book of the Holy Hierotheos*. London, 1927.

This is the principal complete old Syriac edition. The work is transmitted
pseudonymously under Hierotheos.

### Barhadbeshabba 'Arabaya

- Addai Scher, ed. and trans., "La Cause de la fondation des ecoles." Patrologia Orientalis 4.4. Paris, 1907/1908.
- Francois Nau, ed. and trans., "La seconde partie de l'histoire de Barhadbeshabba 'Arbaia et controverse de Theodore de Mopsueste avec les Macedoniens." Patrologia Orientalis 9.5. Paris, 1913.

Only part of the Ecclesiastical History qualifies under this cutoff; the first
part appeared in 1932.

### Sahdona / Martyrius

- Paul Bedjan, ed., *S. Martyrii qui et Sahdona quae supersunt omnia*. Paris/Leipzig, 1902.

This is the principal public-domain collection. It is not purely Sahdona: the
final portion contains homilies of Jacob of Serugh.

### Babai the Great

- A. A. Vaschalde, ed. and trans., *Babai Magni Liber de Unione*, CSCO 79-80, Scriptores Syri 34-35. Paris, 1915.

CSCO 79 contains the Syriac text; CSCO 80 contains the Latin translation.

### Isho'yahb III of Adiabene

- J.-B. Chabot, ed. and trans., "Histoire de Jesus-Sabran, ecrite par Jesus-yab d'Adiabene." *Nouvelles Archives des Missions Scientifiques et Litteraires* 7 (1897): 485-584.
- Rubens Duval, ed. and trans., *Isho'yahb Patriarchae III Liber Epistularum*, CSCO 11-12. Paris/Leipzig, 1904-1905.

Duval's edition is the major corpus source for Isho'yahb's letters. Chabot
supplies the separately transmitted *History of Jesus-Sabran*.

### Isaac of Nineveh

- Paul Bedjan, ed., *Mar Isaacus Ninivita de Perfectione Religiosa*. Leipzig, 1909.

This is the principal public-domain Syriac edition of the traditional First
Part. The rediscovered Second Part is modern and is not included.

### Severus Sebokht

- Eduard Sachau, ed., *Inedita Syriaca*. Vienna, 1870, especially pp. 127-134.
- Francois Nau, ed. and trans., "Le Traite sur l'astrolabe plan de Severe Sabokt." *Journal asiatique*, 9th series, 13 (1899): 56-101, 238-303.
- Francois Nau, "Notes d'astronomie syrienne." *Journal asiatique* (1910).
- Francois Nau, ed. and trans., "Le traite sur les constellations ecrit en 660 par Severe Sebokht, eveque de Qennesrin," first installment, *Revue de l'Orient chretien* 27 (1929-1930): approximately 343-410.

The continuation of the constellation treatise appeared later and should be
checked separately before inclusion.

### John bar Penkaye

- Alphonse Mingana, ed., *Sources Syriaques*, vol. 1. Leipzig, 1907/1908.

This contains Books X-XV of John's *Ktaba d-Resh Melle*; it is a partial
edition, not the entire work.

### Daniel of Salah

- Gustav Diettrich, ed. and trans., *Eine jakobitische Einleitung in den Psalter in Verbindung mit zwei Homilien aus dem grossen Psalmenkommentar des Daniel von Salah*. Giessen, 1901.

This publishes the introduction and the homilies/commentary on the first two
Psalms, not the full Psalter commentary.

### Jacob of Edessa

- George Phillips, ed. and trans., *Scholia on Passages of the Old Testament by Mar Jacob, Bishop of Edessa*. London, 1864.
- William Wright, ed., "Two Epistles of Mar Jacob, Bishop of Edessa." *Journal of Sacred Literature and Biblical Record* 10 (1867): 430-460.
- George Phillips, ed. and trans., *A Letter by Mar Jacob, Bishop of Edessa, on Syriac Orthography; Also a Tract by the Same Author*. London, 1869.
- Carl Kayser, ed. and trans., *Die Canones Jacobs von Edessa*. Leipzig, 1886.
- E. W. Brooks, ed. and trans., "The Chronological Canon of James of Edessa." *Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morgenlaendischen Gesellschaft* 53 (1899): 261-327; corrections/additions in 54 (1900): 100-102.
- Francois Nau, ed., "Les Resolutions canoniques de Jacques d'Edesse." *Le Canoniste contemporain* 27 (1904): 265-276, 366-376, 468-477, 562-572.
- J.-B. Chabot, ed., *Iacobi Edesseni Hexaemeron, seu in Opus Creationis Libri Septem*, Syriac text, CSCO 92, Scriptores Syri 44. Paris, 1928.

Jacob's eligible material is dispersed. Chabot's 1928 Syriac Hexaemeron is a
major target.

### George, Bishop of the Arabs

- Paul de Lagarde, ed., *Analecta Syriaca*. Leipzig/London, 1858, pp. 108-134.
- Victor Ryssel, ed., "Poemi siriaci di Giorgio vescovo degli Arabi." *Atti della R. Accademia dei Lincei*, series 4, vol. 9 (1890): 45-93.
- Victor Ryssel, ed. and trans., "Die astronomischen Briefe Georgs des Araberbischofs." *Zeitschrift fuer Assyriologie* 8 (1893): 1-55.
- J.-B. Chabot, ed., *Iacobi Edesseni Hexaemeron*, CSCO 92. Paris, 1928.

Attribution metadata is important: some poetic material printed under George's
name is now assigned elsewhere.

## Important authors not yet represented by a substantial qualifying edition

The following authors remain important, but this planning list does not
identify a substantial, verified, pre-1931 Syriac author corpus comparable to
the targets above:

- John of Apamea / John the Solitary
- Dadisho' Qatraya
- John of Dalyatha
- Cyrus of Edessa
- Joseph Hazzaya
- Simeon d-Taybutheh
